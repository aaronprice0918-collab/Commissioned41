# COMPETITIVE SWEEP — ILA "Both Sides of Your Money" Claim
**Date:** July 5, 2026 · **Method:** 17 live web searches + site fetches across 6 competitive angles · **Product tested:** ILA (lite.commissioned41.com, $19.99/mo)

---

## TL;DR VERDICT

**The claim SURVIVES — with one word of caution.**

After an adversarial sweep of personal commission trackers, enterprise comp platforms, AI personal-finance apps, gig-income budgeting apps, and AI sales companions, **no product was found that does both sides**: (1) forecasting an individual rep's commission check from their own parsed pay plan + live deals, AND (2) personal-CFO features (safe-to-spend, bills, cash curve) anchored to that forecast. Every competitor found lives on exactly one side of the wall, and most are sold to employers, not to the rep.

Each *ingredient* exists somewhere (pay-plan parsing exists in B2B at QuotaPath; pipeline-based payout forecasting exists at Everstage; safe-to-spend exists at Cleo/Copilot/Forecast Wallet; commission-economy fintech exists at Tongo). **The combination — in one consumer product the rep buys for themselves — appears to be unique.** The caution: absolute "only" claims carry marketing/legal risk and can be falsified overnight by one launch. Recommended hedge phrasings are at the bottom.

---

## THE CLAIM TESTED

> "The only AI companion for commission earners that knows both sides of your money — what you're earning and what you're keeping."

Decomposed into falsifiable parts:
1. AI companion (persona, chat, tool-use hands) — not a dashboard
2. For commission earners specifically (any industry), bought by the individual
3. EARNING side: parses the rep's own pay plan (photo/PDF), forecasts the check from live deals/pipeline/pace
4. KEEPING side: personal CFO (safe-to-spend, bills, goals, 30-day cash curve) anchored to that *forecast*, not a typed-in salary
5. "Only" = no other single product does 3 + 4 together

**Result: no product found satisfying 3 + 4 together. Parts 1–4 individually all have partial precedents (detailed below).**

## CLOSEST THREATS (RANKED)

**1. Tongo (gettongo.com)** — Same thesis ("commission economy has an income-timing problem"), massive real-estate distribution, fintech rails already built. Missing: AI, forecasting, pay-plan parsing, budgeting, and every industry that isn't real estate. If Tongo bolts an AI forecast + budget layer on top of its rails, it becomes the direct competitor. Conversely, ILA's answer to "why not just get an advance?" is "because 3%/30 days is expensive; knowing your number is free."

**2. Everstage Crystal (everstage.com)** — The only shipping product found where a rep forecasts their paycheck from live pipeline. But: employer-sold B2B, SaaS-sales-centric, zero personal finance, and structurally unlikely to sell $19.99 consumer subscriptions against its own enterprise contracts. Threat = they (or QuotaPath, whose AI already ingests comp-plan documents — quotapath.com/blog/introducing-ai-powered-plan-builder) launch a freemium rep-side consumer app. QuotaPath already markets a "free commission tracking app experience."

**3. Cleo / Origin (web.meetcleo.com · useorigin.com)** — Own the AI-money-companion category with millions of users and real AI chat personas. Missing the entire earning side: no deals, no pay plans, income is inferred backward from bank data. Threat = a "variable income mode" that asks "what do you sell and what's your split?" Cleo shipping a commission module would be the fastest way the claim dies.

**4. Piere (piere.com)** — Best-in-class AI for irregular-income *detection* (volatility-aware adaptive budgets). Still backward-looking, no pipeline concept. Watch for a forward-forecast feature.

**5. Solo (worksolo.com)** — Proves forward income prediction + planning works as a consumer product (with a pay *guarantee* — a feature ILA can't match). Gig-only today; expansion into commission sales would be conceptually trivial but data-hard (they'd have no pay-plan or deal data — which is exactly ILA's moat).

## GAPS / RISKS — WHO COULD COPY FASTEST

1. **Tongo** — has the users and the rails; needs only the AI layer. Fastest path to "both sides" for real estate specifically.
2. **QuotaPath/Everstage** — already have pay-plan AI + pipeline forecasting; need only a consumer app + Plaid. Culturally B2B, so slower, but technically 80% there on the earning side.
3. **Cleo** — has the AI persona + bank data + scale; needs deal/pay-plan ingestion. A single "commission mode" feature launch would force ILA to fight on brand and depth.
4. **"Only" is fragile.** The space is moving fast (Origin's AI advisor, Piere's volatility AI, QuotaPath's document-ingesting plan builder all shipped in the last ~18 months). The claim is true as of July 2026 to the limit of an extensive public sweep — but unverifiable in the absolute, and one ProductHunt launch could falsify it.
5. **What can NOT be claimed as unique:** pay-plan document parsing per se (QuotaPath does it, B2B), pipeline-based payout forecasting per se (Everstage Crystal), safe-to-spend (Cleo/Forecast Wallet), commission-economy positioning (Tongo). **The defensible uniqueness is strictly the combination in one rep-purchased AI companion.**

## RECOMMENDED PUBLIC PHRASING

**BOLD (keeps the claim, adds a one-word shield):**
> "The first AI companion for commission earners that knows both sides of your money — what you're earning and what you're keeping."

("First" is historically checkable and doesn't require policing every future launch the way "only" does. Legal teams prefer it; it sounds just as big.)

**SAFE (fully defensible forever):**
> "Payroll tools know what you earned. Budget apps know what you spent. ILA is the AI companion that connects both — it reads your pay plan, watches your deals, and tells you what you're really making and what you can really spend."

**TERRY-PITCH (spoken, 15 seconds):**
> "Every money app on your phone is looking backward — it sees your check *after* it lands and your spending *after* it's gone. ILA reads your actual pay plan, watches your actual deals, and calls your check before payday — then it budgets your life off that number. Earning side, keeping side, one brain. Nobody else has both. We checked."
